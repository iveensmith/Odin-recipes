Afang Soup

The Nigerian Afang soup is native to the Efiks and Ibibios, people of Akwa Ibom and Cross River state og Nigeria, but enjoyed by all Nigerians. It is very nutritious as the soup consists of vegetables. It is prepared with a generous quantity of water leaves and the herbal Okazi leaves.

Ingredients

  • 400g sliced Okazi/Afang leaves
  • 300g water leaves
  • beef, stock fish, kanda and dry fish
  • 2 tablespoon ground crayfish
  • pepper and salt to taste
  • 2 stock cubes

Steps

  1. Fist wash, drain and slice the water leaves into tiny pieces.
  2. Ground or pound the Okazi leaves
  3. Boil the stock fish for some minutes till it gest soft, then add the beef and kanda and boil, also add the dried fish
  4. add palm oil, pepper, grounded crayfish and stock cubes into the pot
  5. add water leaves and allow to simmer for some minutes
  6. Then add the afang leaves and allow to simmer for 3 to 5 minutes
  7. add salt to taste, simmer for 2 minutes and your afang soup is ready... Enjoy
