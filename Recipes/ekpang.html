Ekpang Nkukwo

Ekpang Nkukwo

Ekpang Nkukwo ia a traditional Nigerian dish native to the Efiks and Ibibios. It is widely enjoyed in Cameroon and very popular with the bakweri tribes. It is a very nutritious meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 medium size cocoyams
  • 2 small water yams (Discorea alata L.)
  • 2 small onions
  • 1kg beef
  • 4 bunch cocoyam leaves (as much as needed to wrap the cocoyam and water yam)
  • 1/2 cup unshelled periwinkles
  • Dry fish
  • Fresh shrimps
  • 3 tablespoon grounded crayfish
  • 1 cup pieces stock fish
  • Scent leaves
  • Dry pepper
  • Palm oil
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Table salt

Instructions

  1. Wash the beef and stock fish into a pot, add onions , seasoning cubes, salt and water, boil until they are both soft.
  2. Cut the cooked beef and stock fish into smaller pieces and set aside.
  3. Peel and wash the cocoyam and water yam properly. Cut them into smaller pieces.
  4. Grate or blend the cocoyam and wateryam mixture and set aside.
  5. Mix the grated/blended mixture with little quantity of water to soften it further. Add a pinch of salt and continue mixing.
  6. Rinse the cocoyamleaves properly and tear into medium piecesand set aside.
  7. Scoop a tablespoon of the gated/blended misture and wrap it lightly with a piece of cocoyam leaf (the wraps are shaped cylindrically).
  8. Blend the fresh pepper and set aside.
  9. Wash the fresh shrimps and the periwinkles.
  10. Wash and slice the scent leaves.
  11. Prepare a clean pot for cooking, rubbing palm oil at its sides and base.

Method

  1. Place periwinklesat the base of the oiled pot.
  2. Arrange the wrapped cocoyam fingers in a circle in the oiled pot.
  3. Add 2 cups of water sequentially from the edge of the pot. Allow to boil on low heat.
  4. Add the cooked beef and stock fish, dry fish, shrimps, salt, seasoning cubes and fresh pepper.
  5. Continue heating on low heat.
  6. Shake the pot ater some minutes, this will prevent it from sticking to the side and base of the pot.
  7. Add the meat stock and palm oil and cook for another 10 mins.
  8. Stir with a wooden spoon.
  9. Add crayfish, dry pepper and scent leaves, stir gently and allow to simmer for 5 minutes.
  10. Your Ekpang Nkukwo is ready, serve hot
