Jollof Rice

Nigerian Jollof rice

Nigerian jollof rice is a rich and incredibly tasty west African meal.

It is a very versatile dish, and it is usually made from scratch using Rice, Tomatoes, and other spices.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of rice
  • 3 medium tomatoes, roughly chopped (about 5 ounces each)
  • 1/2 medium scotch bonnet pepper, stem removed
  • 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 3 small red balls pepper, roughly chopped (about 5 ounces each)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon hot ground chile pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon plus 1 heaping teaspoon onion powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme

Steps

  1. Step 1

    in a blender, combine tomatoes, scotch bonnet pepper and onions; puree. pour out half the puree into a bowl; set aside. Add the bell peppers to the puree remaining in the blender and pulse until smooth. Add to the mixture that was set aside and stir to combine

  2. Step 2

    Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add blended vegetable along with the salt, curry powder, ground chile pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, bay leaves, ginger, and thyme

  3. Step 3

    Stir in the rice until well mixed, then reduce the heat to low

  4. Step 4

    Cover pot and let cook rice is al dente, about 35 minutes. Chech after 30 minutes; if ruce is sauce lodged, remove the lid to cook off yhe excess sauce. If rice seems dry, stir in 1 to 2 cups of water. Allow the rice at the bottom of the pot to char a bit to infuse it with a smoky flavor

Enjoy your meal....
